Kenny Jam 2025 - Solar Charged

Game Jam • July 20, 2025 by hutner

This was my first ever game jam. I've always wanted to participate in one, and the Kenney Game Jam 2025 turned out to be the perfect opportunity. The main appeal for me was to just dive into the deep end of game development — I had dabbled with it a bit before but had never actually shipped a game.

One of the restrictions of this jam was that you could only use premade Kenney Assets. That constraint turned out to be a great thing — it removed a huge chunk of time we would have spent on art and let us focus on design and code.

The theme was "Power". We had a lot of ideas early on, but the asset restriction ruled most of them out. We eventually landed on a tower defense game where "Power" serves a dual role — it's both your health and your currency. The name Solar Charged came from the source of that power: solar energy. We implemented a weather system where the day cycle determines how much power you regenerate before the next enemy wave hits at night.

We made the game in Godot, same as all our games. We worked around 35 of the 48 available hours. It was exhausting and the game was still lacking some polish by the end, but I came away with a real respect for game developers who do this all the time. I learned a lot about component-based design, state machines, and why clean modular code matters so much when you're moving fast.

Despite its rough edges, I'm proud of what we built. It plays well enough that the core loop is satisfying, and it set the foundation for everything that came after it.
