WMJ 2025 - Massaquerade

Game Jam • Dec 29, 2025 by hutner

Massaquerade was our third game jam entry, made for the Winter MelonJam 2025. The theme was "Mask". We took that literally — you're at a masquerade ball, masked, and you're the murderer. You have to go around talking to guests, gathering clues, and eventually identify and eliminate your target.

The biggest technical challenge on this one was dialogue. We built a JSON-based dialogue system where you could define flags to set, flags to check, which node to go to next, and a variety of other options. Not only was designing a scalable system like that difficult, but then actually writing all of the dialogue was its own challenge on top of it. In the end, the main limitation we ran into was how fast we could write dialogue rather than how fast we could code features.

That said, I'm proud of the system we built. It's flexible and could carry a much larger game if we revisited it.

The results were strong. Out of 200 submissions we placed 36th overall, with our highest individual category being Graphics at 17th. Gameplay came in at 39th. Given the scope of what we were trying to do in 72 hours, I think that's a really solid result. I wasn't completely happy with the final game — it felt like we ran out of time before the mystery had fully room to breathe — but I still had a great time making it.
